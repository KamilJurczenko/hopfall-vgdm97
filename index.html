💡 The Core Concept

HOP

Die, learn, adapt. Every run reveals a secret.

The twist: holding jump cancels gravity for a brief window — but your momentum carries forward. Master the rhythm or meet the spikes. 🪝

⚙️ Core Mechanics

  • Hop — tap to jump with a fixed arc; hold to float briefly
  • Fall Dash — air-dash downward for speed & gap crossing
  • Wall Catch — cling to surfaces; release resets fall momentum
  • Gravity Wells — environmental hazards that pull or repel the player
  • Rhythm Windows — actions land a bonus multiplier on the beat

All five mechanics emerge from a single physics model — no special-case code.

🕹️ Making it Feel Right

Coyote Time

6 frame grace period after walking off a ledge — players feel skilled, not cheated.

Jump Buffer

8 frame input buffer — forgiving without removing challenge.

Variable Gravity

Gravity scale ramps up mid-fall. Short hops feel snappy; long falls feel weighty.

Screen Shake & Hitstop

Every landing has feedback. Player always knows where they are in space.

🗺️ Level Design Philosophy

"Each level is a sentence. The player reads it left-to-right, then has to write the next one."

Zone 1 — The Ledge: teaches the hop. Flat platforms, no enemies.

Zone 2 — The Abyss: introduces falls. Safety nets below teach commitment.

Zone 3 — The Pulse: rhythm windows unlock. Beat-sync hazards appear.

Zone 4 — Freefall: procedural section. No safe floor. Pure mastery check.

🎨 Visual Style

Aesthetic

High-contrast pixel art with a limited 16-color palette per zone. Dark backgrounds, vivid foreground hazards — clear readability under pressure.

Parallax Layers

3-layer scrolling: background cityscape → midground platforms → foreground particles.

🎵 Sound & Music

  • Adaptive soundtrack — BPM locks to the player's combo streak
  • Procedural SFX layers — each hop pitch-shifts based on height
  • Death jingle is always 1 bar — keeps pace, avoids frustration
  • Silence as a mechanic — Gravity Wells mute the music
"The music doesn't play at you — it plays with you."

🧱 Challenges We Faced

Problem: Physics felt floaty and unpredictable at high frame rates.

→ Fixed timestep + custom gravity multiplier interpolated per frame.

Problem: Rhythm detection drifted after 2–3 minutes of play.

→ Audio clock anchored to a dedicated AudioStreamPlayer node, not game delta.

Problem: Players stopped reading hazards — died "unfairly".

→ Hazard preview flashes 1 beat before activation.

📚 Lessons Learned

  • Prototype the feel first. We spent 3 days on art before the jump felt good — wrong order.
  • One mechanic, many expressions. Every level challenge uses the same five moves — complexity comes from combination.
  • Playtest early, playtest strangers. Friends are polite. Strangers will die on the tutorial and tell you it's broken.
  • Sound is half the game. Muting the audio during testing hid rhythm-readability bugs for weeks.
